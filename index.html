What this tool can and cannot establish

No check here can say “clear”

The strongest result any register check returns is no evidence found. Objects looted from an archaeological site or taken under colonial rule were never inventoried and never reported stolen, so they cannot appear in a stolen-property register at all. For exactly the objects this project exists to worry about, silence is the expected condition — not a reassuring one.

Only one register is actually queried

INTERPOL, the FBI's National Stolen Art File, the Carabinieri TPC archive and the Lost Art Database have no public API. This tool reads what they publish and links you to the official search; it does not search the registers themselves. Wikidata is the one source genuinely queried, and it certifies nothing.

The biggest gap is unfixed

The national heritage authorities of the fourteen source countries the scorer recognises are not covered. That is where the evidence for the motivating cases lives. A low score for a Cambodian sculpture and a low score for a Dutch painting do not mean the same thing.
